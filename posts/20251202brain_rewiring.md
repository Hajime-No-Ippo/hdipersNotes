# The Glitch

Recently, I realized something terrifying and liberating: I stopped caring.

I lost expensive gear. I lived in sub-zero rental rooms. I faced setbacks that would have crushed my ego a year ago. Yet, my emotional response was flatline. Instead of panic, my brain immediately jumped to: "Okay, what's the workaround? Back to code."

I haven't become numb. I've just finally managed to refactor the oldest, buggiest software in existence: the human survival instinct.

At 43, under the extreme pressure of learning Computer Science from scratch in a foreign land, I am running a live experiment on my own neuroplasticity.

# The "Legacy Code"

We are all running outdated software. Our biological brains are designed for the paleolithic era—prioritizing immediate comfort, defending the ego, and avoiding pain at all costs.

This is our "Legacy Code." In a comfortable life, it works fine. But in the trenches of a mid-life pivot, this legacy code becomes fatal errors. It manifests as anxiety about "face," fear of looking stupid, and the desire to quit when things get hard.

To survive, I had to override this system. It wasn't willpower; it was a forced resource reallocation driven by three existential variables:

### 1. The Burned Boats (沉没成本反向利用)
I cannot go back. The bridge to my old life is ashes. When retreat is impossible, the brain stops wasting CPU cycles on "what if" and focuses entirely on "what now."

### 2. The Singularity Window (极度稀缺的时间窗口)
At 43, this opportunity is a statistical anomaly. It will not happen again. This acute awareness of scarcity makes wasting time on emotions feel like an unaffordable luxury.

### 3. The "Lab Rat" Mission (使命感)
It’s no longer just about me getting a job. I am verifying a possibility for every middle-aged person who wants to change but is afraid. I am the lab rat in this experiment of contemporary reinvention.

# The Algorithm

So, how do you actually rewrite the code? It’s not magic; it’s engineering.

**Step 1: Metacognitive Logging (设置断点)**
When I feel anger, ego, or the urge to procrastinate, I don't act. I treat it as a system alert. "Stop. This is the old monkey brain code executing." Observing the emotion, rather than being the emotion, is the first step to detachment.

**Step 2: Refactoring Action (重构行为)**
I override the default response with a modern one.
* **Old Code:** "This is too hard, I'm tired." -> *Action: Scroll phone.*
* **New Code:** "This is hard because I'm leveling up." -> *Action: Break the problem down, write one more line.*

Pain is no longer a stop signal; it's just friction data indicating growth.

# Conclusion: The Ongoing Commit

I haven't reached enlightenment. I'm just extremely busy.

I've optimized my system for one thing: evolution. This requires daily maintenance, high-frequency commits to my habits, and constant debugging of my thoughts.

The experiment continues. The code is compiling.

# 系统故障

最近，我意识到了一个既可怕又自由的事实：我不再在乎了。

我丢过昂贵的装备，住过零度以下的群租房，遭遇过搁在一年前能粉碎我自尊心的挫折。然而，我的情绪反应却是一条直线。我的大脑没有恐慌，而是立即跳到了：“好吧，变通方案是什么？回到代码上来。”

我不是变得麻木了。我只是终于成功重构了现存最古老、Bug 最多的软件：人类的生存本能。

43 岁，在异国他乡从零开始学习计算机科学的极端压力下，我正在拿自己的神经可塑性进行一场活体实验。

# “遗留代码” (Legacy Code)

我们都在运行过时的软件。我们的生物大脑是为旧石器时代设计的——优先考虑眼前的舒适、捍卫自我（面子）、并不惜一切代价避免痛苦。

这就是我们的“遗留代码”。在舒适的生活中，它运行良好。但在中年转型的战壕里，这些遗留代码变成了致命错误。它表现为对“面子”的焦虑、对看起来愚蠢的恐惧，以及在困难时想要放弃的欲望。

为了生存，我必须覆盖这个系统。这不是靠意志力，而是由三个存在主义变量驱动的强制性资源重新分配：

### 1. 破釜沉舟 (The Burned Boats)
我不能回去了。通往我旧生活的桥梁已化为灰烬。当退路已绝，大脑便停止在“如果……会怎样”上浪费 CPU 周期，而完全专注于“现在怎么办”。

### 2. 奇点窗口 (The Singularity Window)
43 岁，这个机会是一个统计学上的异常值。它不会再发生了。这种对稀缺性的敏锐意识，使得把时间浪费在情绪上变成了一种负担不起的奢侈。

### 3. “小白鼠”使命 (The "Lab Rat" Mission)
这不再仅仅关于我找一份工作。我是在为每一个想改变却害怕的中年人验证一种可能性。我是这场当代重塑实验中的小白鼠。

# 算法

那么，实际上如何重写代码呢？这不是魔法；这是工程学。

**步骤 1：元认知日志 (设置断点)**
当我感到愤怒、自负或想要拖延的冲动时，我不会立刻行动。我把它当作一个系统警报。“停。这是旧的猴子大脑代码在执行。” 观察情绪，而不是成为情绪，是抽离的第一步。

**步骤 2：重构行为 (Refactoring Action)**
我用现代的反应覆盖默认的反应。
* **旧代码：** “这太难了，我累了。” -> *动作：刷手机。*
* **新代码：** “这很难，因为我正在升级。” -> *动作：分解问题，再写一行。*

痛苦不再是停止信号；它只是指示成长的摩擦力数据。

# 结论：持续提交

我并没有顿悟成佛。我只是极其忙碌。

我已经优化了我的系统，只为了一个目标：进化。这需要日常维护，对我的习惯进行高频提交 (Commit)，并不间断地 Debug 我的思想。

实验还在继续。代码正在编译。
