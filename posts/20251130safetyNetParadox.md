# The Safety Net Paradox: How My Lawyer License Fuels My Coding Drive

In my last post, I talked about the intense struggle of learning to code at 43, positioning it as a battle between my old self and a new engineering mindset. I ended with the dramatic image of "the gavel striking down, the keyboard lighting up," symbolizing a decisive break from the past.

But there's a crucial piece of the puzzle I didn't mention—a secret weapon that fundamentally changes the psychological game of this career pivot.

**I am still a licensed lawyer. I can go back to practicing law at any time.**

This fact might seem like it would make me complacent. After all, if the coding thing doesn't work out, I have a solid, respectable career waiting for me. Why push myself to the limit?

This is what I call the **"Safety Net Paradox."**

For many, a "Plan B" is a soft sofa to fall back into at the first sign of difficulty. But for me, my legal license is not a sofa; it's a **trampoline**.

Knowing that my livelihood and identity don't *solely* depend on becoming a programmer gives me a kind of super-power: **fearlessness**.

* When a concept like recursion or dynamic programming breaks my brain, I don't spiral into panic about being unemployable. I can afford the time to sit with the confusion until it clicks.
* I don't have to chase the quickest, dirtiest path to a junior developer job just to pay the bills. I can focus on building a rock-solid foundation and aiming for work that genuinely interests me, like the intersection of law and tech.
* This safety net doesn't reduce the pressure; it transforms it. The pressure is no longer existential dread; it's the pure, clean pressure of ambition. I'm not coding to survive; I'm coding because I *choose* to, and that choice is incredibly empowering.

My legal background isn't dead weight. It's the solid ground beneath my feet that allows me to leap higher and take bigger risks in this new world.

# 安全网悖论：我的律师执照如何成为我编程的动力

在上一篇文章中，我谈到了 43 岁学习编程的激烈挣扎，把它比作我的旧自我与新工程思维之间的一场战斗。我用“法槌敲下，键盘亮起”这样一个充满戏剧性的画面作为结尾，象征着与过去的决裂。

但我没有提到拼图中至关重要的一块——一个从根本上改变了这场职业转型心理博弈的秘密武器。

**我仍然是一名持有执照的律师。我可以随时回去执业。**

这个事实似乎会让我变得自满。毕竟，如果编程这条路走不通，我还有一个稳固、体面的职业在等着我。为什么还要把自己逼到极限呢？

这就是我所说的**“安全网悖论”**。

对许多人来说，“B 计划”是一个在遇到困难时可以退回去的舒适沙发。但对我来说，我的律师执照不是沙发，它是一个**蹦床**。

知道我的生计和身份并不*完全*依赖于成为一名程序员，这给了我一种超能力：**无所畏惧**。

* 当递归或动态规划这样的概念让我的大脑死机时，我不会因为担心失业而陷入恐慌。我有足够的时间与困惑相处，直到我真正理解它。
* 我不必为了支付账单而被迫选择一条最快、最粗糙的路径去寻找一份初级开发人员的工作。我可以专注于打下坚实的基础，并以真正让我感兴趣的工作为目标，比如法律和技术的交叉领域。
* 这个安全网并没有减少压力，而是转化了压力。这种压力不再是生存的恐惧，而是纯粹、干净的野心。我不是为了生存而编程；我编程是因为我*选择*这样做，这种选择赋予了我巨大的力量。

我的法律背景不是累赘。它是我脚下坚实的土地，让我在这个新世界里跳得更高，承担更大的风险。
