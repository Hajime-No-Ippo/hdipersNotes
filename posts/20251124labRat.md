# The Lab Rat in the Mirror

## 1. Trial and Error
Lately, I feel less like a student and more like a scientist conducting an experiment. The subject of this experiment? Myself.

I used to believe that "more time equals more progress." So I pushed myself to the limit—sleeping three hours, drinking endless energy drinks. The result was not efficiency, but a system crash.
Now, I am trying a different approach. I am testing my brain's "battery life." I found that my focus peaks for about 45 minutes before it needs a reset. I realized that rest isn't a pause button; it's a recharge station.

[END]

[ZH]
# 镜子里的实验鼠

## 1. 试错
最近，我觉得自己不太像个学生，倒更像是个正在做实验的科学家。而这个实验的对象？就是我自己。

以前我迷信“时间堆砌”，觉得只要熬的时间够长，学的东西就够多。于是我试过每天只睡三小时，靠功能饮料续命。结果呢？换来的不是高效，而是系统的崩溃。
现在，我在尝试另一种路子。我在测试大脑的“续航极限”。我发现我的专注力大概只能维持45分钟的高峰，然后就需要重启。我开始意识到，休息不是暂停键，而是充电站。
[END]

[EN]
## 2. From Rote Memorization to Internalization
Another experiment is about *how* I remember things.
When I first encountered algorithms like "Monotonic Queue," I tried to memorize the code line by line. It felt like carrying sand in a sieve—it kept slipping away.
Yesterday, I tried something new: "Chunking." Instead of memorizing the syntax, I memorized the *logic blocks*—clean, maintain, add, record.
Suddenly, the code wasn't a burden; it became a natural flow. It turns out my brain doesn't like data; it likes stories and structures.

[END]

[ZH]
## 2. 从死记硬背到内化
另一个实验关于“记忆”。
刚接触像“单调队列”这种算法时，我试图一行行背代码。那感觉就像用筛子装沙子，怎么都留不住。
昨天，我试了个新法子：“逻辑分块”。我不背语法了，我背逻辑模块——清理、维护、入队、记录。
突然间，代码不再是负担，而变成了一种自然的流淌。原来我的大脑不爱记枯燥的数据，它喜欢故事和结构。
[END]

[EN]
## 3. Still Calibrating
I haven't found the perfect formula yet. Some days I still overwork; some days I feel inefficient.
But that's okay. The point isn't to be a perfect machine from day one. The point is the calibration.
I am observing my reactions, adjusting my inputs, and analyzing the outputs. I am not just learning Computer Science; I am learning the user manual for my own mind.
It’s a quiet, private process, but perhaps the most important one I’ll ever undertake.

[END]

[ZH]
## 3. 仍在校准中
我还没找到那个完美的公式。有时候我还是会用力过猛，有时候又觉得自己效率太低。
但这没关系。重点不在于第一天就变成一台完美的机器，重点在于“校准”。
我在观察自己的反应，调整输入，然后分析输出。我不仅仅是在学计算机科学，我其实是在一边摸索，一边编写我自己这颗大脑的“用户手册”。
这是一个安静、私密的过程，但或许也是我这辈子做过的最重要的事。
[END]
